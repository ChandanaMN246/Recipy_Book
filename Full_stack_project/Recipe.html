Slow Cooker Butter Chicken

  • Time: 4 h 30 m
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
Butter 2 tbs
Vegetable Oil 2 tbs
Skinless, boneless chicken thighs 4 thighs
Onion 1 onion
Garlic 3 cloves
Curry Powder 2 tsp
Curry Paste 1 tbs
Tandoori Masala 2 tsp
Garam Masala 1 tsp
Tomato Paste 1 can (6 oz)
Green Cardamom Pods 15 pods
Low Fat Plain Yogurt 1 cup
Coconut Milk 1 can (14 oz)

Step 1: Melt the butter and vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Stir in the chicken, onion, and garlic. Cook and stir until the onion has softened and turned translucent, about 10 minutes. Stir in the curry powder, curry paste, tandoori masala, garam masala, and tomato paste until no lumps of tomato paste remain. Pour into a slow cooker, and stir in the cardamom pods, coconut milk, and yogurt. Season to taste with salt.

Step 2: Cook on High 4 to 6 hours, or on Low 6 to 8 hours until the chicken is tender and the sauce has reduced to your desired consistency. Remove and discard the cardamom pods before serving.

Slow Cooker Flank Steak Fajitas

  • Time: 8 h
  • Serves: 8
Ingredients Quantity
Beef Flank Steak 1 steak (2 lbs)
Green Pepper 1 medium pepper, cut into strips
Sweet Red Pepper 1 medium pepper, cut into strips
Onion 1 medium onion, halved and sliced
Fajita Seasoning Mix 1 envelope
Beer 1/2 cup
Flour Tortillas 8 tortillas (warmed)
Pico de Gallo 1 cup

Step 1: Place first five ingredients in a 5-qt. slow cooker. Pour beer over top. Cook, covered, on low until meat is tender, 6-8 hours.

Step 2: Remove beef from slow cooker. Strain vegetable mixture; return vegetables to slow cooker. (Discard cooking juices or save for another use.) Shred beef with two forks; add to vegetables and heat through. Serve in tortillas with pico de gallo and cilantro.

Easy cherry Gelato

  • Time: 4 hours
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
Fresh Cherries 2 cups, pitted
Granulated Sugar 1/2 cup
Whole Milk 2 cups
Heavy Cream 1 cup
Vanilla Extract 1 teaspoon

Step 1: In a blender or food processor, combine the fresh cherries and granulated sugar. Blend until smooth.

Step 2: In a mixing bowl, whisk together the cherry mixture, whole milk, heavy cream, and vanilla extract until well combined.

Step 3: Pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer's instructions, usually for about 20-25 minutes, or until the gelato reaches a soft, creamy consistency.

Step 4: Transfer the gelato to a lidded container and freeze for at least 3-4 hours to firm up.

Step 5: Serve the cherry gelato in bowls or cones and enjoy!

Orecchiette, Chickpeas, and Cherry Tomatoes

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Orecchiette Pasta 12 ounces
Olive Oil 2 tablespoons
Garlic 3 cloves, minced
Cherry Tomatoes 2 cups, halved
Chickpeas 1 can (15 ounces), drained and rinsed
Fresh Basil 1/4 cup, chopped
Red Pepper Flakes 1/4 teaspoon (optional)
Salt To taste
Black Pepper To taste
Grated Parmesan Cheese For serving

Step 1: Cook the orecchiette pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.

Step 2: In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute, until fragrant.

Step 3: Add the halved cherry tomatoes to the skillet and cook for 3-4 minutes, until they start to soften and release their juices.

Step 4: Stir in the chickpeas, chopped fresh basil, and red pepper flakes (if desired). Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes to heat the chickpeas through.

Step 5: Add the cooked orecchiette pasta to the skillet and toss well to coat the pasta with the tomato and chickpea mixture. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.

Step 6: Serve the orecchiette, chickpeas, and cherry tomatoes in bowls, garnished with grated Parmesan cheese. Enjoy!

Chilaquiles

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Corn Tortillas 12 tortillas, stale or day-old
Vegetable Oil 1/4 cup
Onion 1 medium, diced
Garlic 2 cloves, minced
Red or Green Salsa 1 1/2 cups
Chicken or Vegetable Broth 1/2 cup
Shredded Chicken or Cooked Beans 2 cups
Queso Fresco or Feta Cheese 1/2 cup, crumbled
Cilantro 1/4 cup, chopped
Sour Cream For serving

Step 1: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Cut the tortillas into triangles or strips.

Step 2: In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Fry the tortilla pieces in batches until crispy and golden brown. Remove them from the skillet and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.

Step 3: In the same skillet, sauté the diced onion and minced garlic until softened and fragrant.

Step 4: Add the red or green salsa to the skillet, followed by the chicken or vegetable broth. Stir to combine.

Step 5: Add the shredded chicken or cooked beans to the skillet and simmer for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

Step 6: Remove the skillet from heat and gently fold in the fried tortilla pieces until they are evenly coated with the salsa mixture.

Step 7: Transfer the chilaquiles to a baking dish and sprinkle crumbled queso fresco or feta cheese on top.

Step 8: Bake in the preheated oven for 10-15 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Step 9: Remove from the oven and garnish with chopped cilantro. Serve hot with a dollop of sour cream on top.

Tacos Al Pastor

  • Time: 2 hours 30 minutes
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
Pork Shoulder 2 pounds, thinly sliced
Pineapple 1/2 cup, finely chopped
Onion 1 medium, finely chopped
Garlic 3 cloves, minced
Achiote Paste 2 tablespoons
White Vinegar 2 tablespoons
Pineapple Juice 1/2 cup
Dried Oregano 1 teaspoon
Cumin 1 teaspoon
Salt To taste
Corn Tortillas For serving
Chopped Cilantro For garnish
Diced Onion For garnish
Pineapple Slices For garnish

Step 1: In a blender or food processor, combine the chopped pineapple, onion, minced garlic, achiote paste, white vinegar, pineapple juice, dried oregano, cumin, and salt. Blend until smooth to make the marinade.

Step 2: Place the thinly sliced pork shoulder in a large bowl and pour the marinade over it. Make sure the pork is well coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results.

Step 3: Preheat your grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Remove the marinated pork from the refrigerator and thread the slices onto skewers.

Step 4: Grill the pork skewers for about 5-7 minutes per side, or until cooked through and slightly charred.

Step 5: Remove the pork from the skewers and let it rest for a few minutes. Then, thinly slice the grilled pork.

Step 6: Heat the corn tortillas on a griddle or in a dry skillet until warmed through and slightly toasted.

Step 7: Assemble your Tacos Al Pastor by placing a few slices of grilled pork on each warm tortilla. Garnish with chopped cilantro, diced onion, and a few pineapple slices.

Step 8: Serve the Tacos Al Pastor with your favorite salsa or hot sauce on the side.

Source: tasty.com

Kung Pao Chicken

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Chicken Breast 1 pound, boneless and skinless, diced
Vegetable Oil 2 tablespoons
Dried Red Chilies 8-10 chilies
Garlic 3 cloves, minced
Ginger 1-inch piece, minced
Green Bell Pepper 1 medium, diced
Red Bell Pepper 1 medium, diced
Roasted Peanuts 1/2 cup
Scallions (Green Onions) 4, sliced
Soy Sauce 2 tablespoons
Rice Vinegar 1 tablespoon
Hoisin Sauce 1 tablespoon
Sesame Oil 1 teaspoon
Cornstarch 1 teaspoon
Salt To taste
Cooked Rice For serving

Step 1: In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch, and a pinch of salt. Set the sauce aside.

Step 2: Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over high heat. Add dried red chilies and stir-fry for about 30 seconds until they become fragrant.

Step 3: Add minced garlic and ginger to the skillet and stir-fry for another 30 seconds.

Step 4: Add the diced chicken breast to the skillet and stir-fry until it turns golden brown and is cooked through.

Step 5: Add diced green and red bell peppers to the skillet and stir-fry for about 2-3 minutes until they become slightly tender.

Step 6: Pour the sauce mixture into the skillet and stir to coat the chicken and vegetables evenly. Cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens.

Step 7: Add roasted peanuts and sliced scallions to the skillet and toss everything together.

Step 8: Remove from heat and serve the Kung Pao Chicken over cooked rice.

Dumplings

  • Time: 1 hour
  • Serves: 4-6
Ingredients Quantity
Ground Pork 1 pound
Cabbage 2 cups, finely chopped
Green Onion 1/2 cup, finely chopped
Ginger 1 tablespoon, minced
Garlic 2 cloves, minced
Soy Sauce 2 tablespoons
Sesame Oil 1 tablespoon
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Black Pepper 1/4 teaspoon
Wonton Wrappers 1 package (about 50 wrappers)

Step 1: In a large bowl, combine ground pork, chopped cabbage, chopped green onion, minced ginger, minced garlic, soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and black pepper. Mix well until all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.

Step 2: Take one wonton wrapper at a time and place a spoonful of the pork filling in the center of the wrapper.

Step 3: Wet the edges of the wrapper with water and fold it in half to form a triangle. Press the edges firmly to seal the dumpling. Repeat this process with the remaining wrappers and filling.

Step 4: Place the formed dumplings on a baking sheet or tray lined with parchment paper to prevent sticking.

Step 5: In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the dumplings in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pot. Cook the dumplings for about 5-7 minutes or until they float to the surface and the filling is cooked through.

Step 6: Remove the cooked dumplings with a slotted spoon and serve them hot with dipping sauces like soy sauce, vinegar, or chili oil.

Sushi

  • Time: 1 hour
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Sushi Rice 2 cups
Nori Sheets 4 sheets
Sushi-grade Fish 8 ounces, thinly sliced
Cucumber 1, julienned
Avocado 1, sliced
Soy Sauce For dipping
Wasabi For serving
Pickled Ginger For serving

Step 1: Prepare sushi rice according to package instructions and let it cool.

Step 2: Place a sheet of nori on a bamboo sushi mat.

Step 3: Wet your hands and grab a handful of sushi rice. Spread the rice evenly on the nori sheet, leaving about an inch at the top free of rice.

Step 4: Arrange the sliced fish, cucumber, and avocado in a line across the center of the rice.

Step 5: Roll the sushi tightly using the bamboo mat, applying gentle pressure as you roll.

Step 6: Repeat the process for the remaining nori sheets and ingredients.

Step 7: Slice each sushi roll into bite-sized pieces.

Guay Teow (Noodle Soup)

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Rice Noodles 8 ounces
Chicken Breast 8 ounces, boneless and skinless, thinly sliced
Shrimp 8 ounces, peeled and deveined
Bok Choy 2 cups, chopped
Bean Sprouts 1 cup
Green Onion 1/2 cup, sliced
Cilantro 1/4 cup, chopped
Garlic 3 cloves, minced
Ginger 1 tablespoon, grated
Chicken Broth 4 cups
Soy Sauce 3 tablespoons
Fish Sauce 2 tablespoons
Lime 1, cut into wedges
Chili Sauce 1 tablespoon

Step 1: Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.

Step 2: In a large pot, heat some oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and grated ginger, and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant.

Step 3: Add the sliced chicken breast to the pot and cook until no longer pink.

Step 4: Pour the chicken broth into the pot and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to a simmer.

Step 5: Add the peeled and deveined shrimp, chopped bok choy, bean sprouts, and sliced green onions to the pot. Cook for about 2-3 minutes until the shrimp turns pink and the vegetables are slightly tender.

Step 6: Stir in soy sauce and fish sauce. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste preferences.

Step 7: Divide the cooked rice noodles among serving bowls. Ladle the hot soup over the noodles.

Step 8: Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve with lime wedges and chili sauce on the side.

Khao Pad (Fried Rice)

  • Time: 20 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients Quantity
Cooked Rice 4 cups, preferably day-old rice
Chicken Breast 8 ounces, boneless and skinless, diced
Shrimp 8 ounces, peeled and deveined
Eggs 2
Carrots 1/2 cup, diced
Peas 1/2 cup
Onion 1/2 cup, diced
Garlic 2 cloves, minced
Vegetable Oil 3 tablespoons
Soy Sauce 2 tablespoons
Fish Sauce 1 tablespoon
Oyster Sauce 1 tablespoon
Sugar 1 teaspoon
Green Onion 2 stalks, sliced
Cilantro 2 tablespoons, chopped

Step 1: In a large wok or skillet, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and diced onion, and stir-fry for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant.

Step 2: Add diced chicken breast to the wok and stir-fry until cooked through and slightly browned.

Step 3: Push the chicken to one side of the wok and crack the eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until they are almost fully cooked.

Step 4: Add diced carrots and peas to the wok and stir-fry for about 2 minutes until the vegetables are slightly tender.

Step 5: Add the peeled and deveined shrimp to the wok and stir-fry until they turn pink and are cooked through.

Step 6: Add the cooked rice to the wok and gently break up any clumps. Stir-fry the rice with the other ingredients for about 3-4 minutes until heated through.

Step 7: In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, fish sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar. Pour the sauce mixture over the rice and stir-fry for an additional 1-2 minutes until the rice is evenly coated.

Step 8: Remove from heat and garnish with sliced green onions and chopped cilantro. Serve the Khao Pad hot as a main dish or as a side dish.
